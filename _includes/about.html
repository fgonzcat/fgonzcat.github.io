Physics

I specialize in the physics of high-pressure and high-temperature matter, focusing on planetary interiors. Combining condensed matter physics with thermodynamics, I use density functional theory (DFT) to perform ab initio molecular dynamics and electronic structure calculations to build equations of state (EOS) of materials that are relevant for planetary and exoplanetary interiors. These EOS are crucial for understanding the mineralogy and geophysics at extreme conditions, allowing us to interpret astronomical observations and enhance our models of planetary structure and evolution. In addition, I study the properties of warm dense matter (dense plasmas) on the pathway to fusion conditions.

Code & Tools

My coding expertise spans C++, Python, Bash, and Awk. In my research, I also integrate tools like VASP, LAMMPS, OpenGL, Git, and OVITO to perform complex simulations and visualize atomic-level phenomena. These skills and tools enable me to derive equations of state for materials, critical for modeling planetary interiors where experimental data is scarce.

Planetary Science

New physics of planetary interiors include the core crystallization of super-Earths, the discovery of the erosion of Jupiter's core, miscibility of ice-rock mixtures at planetary interiors, double-superionicity of planetary ices, and stability of iron phases at the core of exoplanets.
